Digital Tools for Environmental Investigations

About the Panel: Uncovering Environmental Stories

Environmental crimes often involve complex networks spanning remote locations and multiple jurisdictions, making them difficult to investigate. This session from GIJC25 demonstrates how journalists can use cutting-edge digital tools—including AI-powered satellite analysis, geospatial mapping, and data linking—to expose hidden environmental destruction and hold power to account.

Key Takeaways for Reporters

Data is Your Tip

Alerts, disclosures, or changes in land and financial data are often the first sign of a story. Build systematic monitoring into your workflow instead of waiting for a source.

Link People, Places, Money

The story often lies in the network. The same actors appear across multiple infractions and supply chains. Visualizing these links creates a more compelling narrative.

Make Data Human

Beyond charts and maps, show the human impact. Who is being harmed? Who is benefiting? Connect the data to policy failures, environmental risks, and community consequences.

Five Core Investigative Techniques

1. Investigating Climate Adaptation

Move beyond disaster reporting to focus on solutions and accountability. Use databases and public records to track climate adaptation funding, project implementation, and real-world outcomes, exposing failures and "maladaptation."

2. Database Linking

This is the foundation of the story. Connect disparate datasets—such as environmental violation records, corporate registries, land titles, and political donations—to uncover hidden relationships, conflicts of interest, and systemic issues.

3. Network Analysis

Once datasets are linked, use graph tools to visualize the connections between companies, owners, and infractions. This technique reveals patterns of repeat offenders, hidden ownership structures, and political influence that are invisible in spreadsheets.

4. Story Building & Verification

Transform data leads into a narrative. Use satellite imagery time-series, official documents, and Freedom of Information requests to verify findings. When possible, on-the-ground field reporting is crucial to confirm digital leads and add human context.

5. Replicability & Adaptation

The methods are universal, even if the specific databases differ. Adapt the workflow to your country by identifying key local data sources for land, corporate, and environmental records. The core principle of "spot change → trace actor → link finance" is globally transferable.

Recommended Tools & Platforms

A powerful workflow combines tools for each stage of the investigation, from lead generation to publication.

Satellite & Geospatial Analysis

  • Planet Explorer: High-resolution, fresh satellite imagery to detect new clearings or infrastructure.
  • Sentinel Hub EO Browser: Free access to Sentinel & Landsat time-series imagery to track changes over time.
  • Google Earth Pro: Desktop tool for visualizing historical imagery and building story visuals.
  • QGIS: Open-source GIS for advanced mapping and spatial data analysis.

Data Platforms & Registries

  • CruzaGrafos: A Brazilian platform built by ABRAJI / Brasil.IO that allows users to visualise networks of companies, landowners, infractions and share-holders in Brazil.
  • Sayari Graph: Commercial platform for mapping corporate, trade, and supply-chain data.
  • Aleph (OCCRP): Free investigative data platform to cross-reference public records, leaks, and company registries.
  • Trase.earth: Maps global supply chains of agricultural commodities and their deforestation links.

Data Processing & Visualization

  • OpenRefine: A free tool for cleaning and transforming messy datasets.
  • Pinpoint (Google): Uses AI to extract people, places, and organizations from large document sets.
  • Datawrapper: Quickly create charts, maps, and interactive visualizations for publication.
  • Pandas (Python library): For advanced data analysis, filtering, and aggregation.

Publication & Verification

  • DocumentCloud: Upload, annotate, and share public documents for transparency.
  • CruzaGrafos: A Brazilian tool to visualize networks of companies, landowners, and infractions.
  • NotebookLM (Google): An AI assistant to help you research and summarize your own uploaded documents.
